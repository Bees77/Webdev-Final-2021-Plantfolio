Why is Botany important

Plants make up 82% of our global biomass, and they have more biodiversity than any animal species. Botany is the study of plants, how they grow, and conservation of endangered species. Without plants, everything would die, and with decreasing biodiversity as a result of human activities, we need to learn more about botanical sciences so we can curb our behaviors that damage ecosystems, conserve endangere species, and preserve biodiversity.

Invasive plants

Invasive plants are a huge issue, especially in Texas. They disturb local ecosystems by competing with native plant populations for resources, genetically evolving more quickly than surrounding organisms, and killing plants that are a part of native animals' diets, which can cause overall population decline in anything that iss livng, which is bad. Some invasive plants spread naturally, but a lot are spread by human activity. Rosemary is an example of an invasive herb in Texas, and it should only be grown in pots.

Deforestation

Deforestation is an entirely human activity that causees catastrophic damage to ecosystems and botanical communities.

Irresponsible Imports

A new market for houseplants has arrisen in recent years, and while this is great and benefits both plants and gardeners, and gives people in urban areas access to botany, it has also sparked an illegal trade of exotic plants across oceans and biomes, which can cause environmental havoc. This can bring invasive plants and insects into ecosystems that have no place for them, leading to imbalance of food and water chains and overpopulation, as well as the spreading of pathogens. Make sure you buy your plants from responsible growers, and pay attention to whether a plant on the market is an endangered species.
