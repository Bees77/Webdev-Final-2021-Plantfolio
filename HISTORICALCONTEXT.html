Botanical Pioneers

George Washington Carver

George Washington Carver is probably one of the biggest revolutionaries in American Agriculture to date, and the most influential American botanist.

Gregor Mendel

Perhaps one of the most famous botanists, Gregor Mendel was a friar who used pea plant chromosomes to establish the foundational theory of genetics in western science.

Katherine Essau

Katherine Essau was a German-American scientist given a National Medal of Science for her botanical work.

Emma Lucy Braun

Emma Lucy Braun was a prominant arborialist and botanist in the U.S.

Janaki Ammal

Janaki Ammal was one of the first women to attain a PhD in botany in the U.S., and her research contributed greatly to our current knowledge of chromosomes in agricultural crops.

Ynes Mexia

Ynes Mexia was one of the most successful botanical journalists and collectors of her time, and travelled across the americas to find her specimens.

Agriculture vs. Botany

Agriculture and Botany are often considered to be two branches within the same discipline, and to an extent this is true, but they have different focuses and histories. Historically, botanists are known for discovery and research while agriculturalists for cultivation of specific crops. Both require skill, and often work together, but unfortuneately agriculture usually does not take a very holistic approach. With a move towards mroe sustainable agrivulture, permaculture, and reforestation, that may change for the better, but when talking about the history of botanical exploration and agricultural cultivation it is important to diffrentiate.

Botanical Art and Illustration

Botanical Art and Illustration have a long history and are essential to the study of botany. Botanical art is usually made for aesthetic purposes with identification in mind, but botanical illustration is always made scientifically. In Europe, Botanical Art was essential to the funding of exploration expeditions. Court artists for kings would often join scientists, or take up the sciences themselves, and bring back valuable pieces for royals, incentivising them to continue funding exploration.
